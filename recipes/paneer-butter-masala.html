Paneer Butter Masala Recipe


Description:

Paneer Butter Masala (also known as Paneer Makhani) is a rich, creamy North Indian curry. It features soft, pillowy paneer cubes simmered in a velvety, slightly sweet, and mildly spiced tomato-cashew gravy. It pairs perfectly with garlic naan, butter roti, or fragrant jeera rice.


Key Ingredients:

    For the Gravy:

    • 3-4 medium tomatoes, roughly chopped
    • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
    • 10-15 raw cashews (for creaminess)
    • 1.5 inches ginger & 5-6 garlic cloves, chopped
    • 1 tbsp oil & 2 tbsp unsalted butter
    • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
    • /2 tsp garam masala
    • 1 tsp sugar (to balance the tangy tomatoes)
    • 3/4 tsp salt

    For the Paneer:

    • 250g paneer, cut into cubes
    • 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves, crushed)
    • 3-4 tbsp heavy cream

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Boil the Base:

    In a pan, add the onions, ginger, garlic, tomatoes, cashews, and 1 cup of water. Cook covered on medium heat for 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are soft and mushy. Cool and blend into a smooth puree.

  2. Spice the Base:

    Heat the oil and butter in the pan. Add the Kashmiri red chili powder (this gives the gravy its signature bright color) and fry for a few seconds. Pour in the blended paste.

  3. Simmer:

    Add salt, sugar, and garam masala. Let the curry simmer for 5 minutes on low heat.

  4. Finish:

    Add the paneer cubes and crushed kasuri methi. Cover and cook for 2-3 minutes. Turn off the heat and stir in the heavy cream.
